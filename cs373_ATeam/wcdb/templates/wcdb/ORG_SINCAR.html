History
Founded in 1989, the Sinaloa Cartel has become the most powerful cartel in the last few years.2 It's success has come in part from infiltrating and bribing Mexican officals and being less confrontational than other cartels.1 While minimizing direct conflict with the Mexican government, the Sinaloa Cartel is at war with rival cartels, including Los Zetas, and is a big part of the Mexican drug violence.
Its leader is Joaquin Guzman Loera, Mexico's most wanted man.
In addition to weed and cocaine, the Sinaloas control 80% of the US meth trade.3

Economic Impact
As a result of the United States Government's decision to implement an extensive surveillance program not only on its own citizens, but also on foreign allies, there has been significant damage to the USA's relations with other countries. As a result, relations between them have become strained, resulting in countries becoming less willing to encourage economic interactions with America. In addition, fear that the US government may be spying on them has led many European businesses and entities associated with European governments to drop or consider dropping American telecommunications service providers such as Verizon and AT&T.1
Human Impact
By conducting massive surveillance operations over hundreds of millions of Americans and millions of others overseas and insisting that it has the right to do so whenever it feels American interests may be involved, the United States Government has violated the Fourth Amendment of the United States Constitution.2 The willingness of Congress, the Executive Branch, and even the Courts to go along with this program bodes ill for the future of our civil liberties.3 While it likely can be truthfully said that this surveillance program is not currently being abused to do things like shut down political opposition, the US Government has a history of doing such things in the past, and blindly trusting that such immense power would never be abused or wielded against the interests of the American people will not end well.
